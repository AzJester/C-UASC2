How to read & drive the COP
A leadership demo of any-sensor / any-shooter C-UAS C2. Not a fielded system.
Symbology
◆ Red diamond — hostile / suspect threat track
◯ Blue circle — friendly air (CAP, ISR, lift); armed types shoot
◆ Green diamond — effector (gun, interceptor, EW, laser, net)
▢ Square — sensor (radar, RF, EO/IR, ship)
◯ White ring — the defended asset (ring tints as integrity drops)
cyan / amber / violet beam — air intercept / kinetic / directed-energy shot
dashed coastline / border — terrain; naval assets sit offshore in blue water
red dashed circle — no-fire zone: kinetic fires prohibited over it; soft-kill (EW / RF takeover / net) only
hatched dark wedge — terrain mask: the ridge line (Point Loma, Franklin Mountains) blinds that sensor to LOW targets beyond it; elevated sensors and other angles cover the gap
Controls
AO — switch scenario (San Diego coast / El Paso border / Norfolk and Hampton Roads / Guam joint-defense sector)
Time — pause/resume and 0.5x / 1x / 2x speed
Inject — choose a joint-defense, airport-defense, or network-resilience AutoBrief lasting 2, 5, 10, or 15 minutes; or inject a swarm / mass raid manually
Weapons Free — requires a second press on the flashing red confirmation. Once armed, eligible ground, aircraft, and naval effectors attack HOSTILE tracks while identity, quality, range, and no-fire gates remain active.
Operate — swap a radar vendor, federate up-echelon, drop a C2 node
Review — the operational (theater) rollup and the After Action Review
Operator wings — priority tracks and posture stay on the left, decision support stays on the right, and the complete AO scales into the center map. Both wings are no-scroll glance views.
San Diego airport — notional KSAN arrivals/departures publish synthetic Mode-S / ADS-B callsign, ICAO address, squawk, quality, altitude, speed, and flight-plan fields. Nearby airport-defense effectors are soft-kill / capture demonstrations inside no-fire constraints.
San Clemente link — the San Diego map includes a visible off-scale island inset where the labeled 5G microwave backhaul terminates. It is illustrative communications symbology, not real infrastructure or coverage.
5G node details — select a purple gNB or POP to open its contextual network card; select it again or click elsewhere to dismiss it.
Airport defense — every scenario airport has arriving/departing transponder traffic, nearby sensors, and soft-kill / capture effectors inside its no-fire constraints.
El Paso border — three labeled Border Patrol stations distribute paired sensors and non-kinetic effectors along the boundary.
Norfolk maritime — naval and commercial surface tracks share Hampton Roads; the 5G backbone terminates at a labeled terrestrial point on land.
Guam joint defense — the satellite-preferred whole-island view uses a public island outline. Fixed sensors, effectors, protected sites, and the 5G fiber path remain on land; only ships are intentionally offshore.
Basemap — SAT · EXTERNAL is the default and requests public Esri World Imagery. TACTICAL · LOCAL remains available manually and is used automatically when imagery is unreachable.
Weather — CLEAR / WIND / RAIN: wind slows small UAS, rain shortens EO/IR and directed-energy reach
Time of day — AUTO follows the scenario clock; NIGHT darkens the plot and cuts EO/IR to 55% (watch its ring shrink)
Degrade comms — DDIL inject: the COP goes stale (positions step every 2.5 s, TQ drops) and engagement orders add latency
Demo audio — presentation-only alert tones and callouts derived from recorded events (declaration, release, BDA, leaker, denial). It does not invent radio, fuel, or readiness state. Off by default.
Audit Log · J-SERIES — the same event feed restated as stylized Link-16 J-series lines (demo flavor, not certified TADIL-J); switch back with LOG

The one-click story
Choose an AutoBrief mission and duration, then press Auto-play brief. It runs hands-free: detection, fusion, the track-quality gate, remote tasking, a visibly confirmed simulated WEAPONS FREE state, joint ground / air / naval engagement, continued waves for the selected time, and outcomes. Any click hands you control.
Flip Architecture to Hub & Spoke to see the legacy model: fixed pairs, idle effectors, coverage gaps. Flip back and the gaps close.
Sensing uses modality-aware notional rules: radar loses range against low flyers, RF cannot see comms-silent drones, and EO/IR needs a cue and degrades at night or in rain. EW is a soft-kill that can be resisted or recovered from; directed energy dwells one target at a time; every shot carries a notional cost and the AAR totals the exchange. Watch for decoys, ISR orbiters, and cruise profiles with dog-legs.
